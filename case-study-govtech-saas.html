GovTech SaaS
Outbound

I built an outbound system for a company selling sales intelligence software into the government tech space. The goal was simple: find the right accounts, score them, and write the outreach without a human doing research on every record.

I pulled 50 companies and scored each one on four signals: funding stage, growth, public sector fit, and what sales tools they already ran. Each company got a score out of 15. The enrichment ran through a waterfall, so if one source came back empty it tried the next instead of just failing. 46 of the 50 decision-makers came back fully enriched.

The email writing was split into pieces. The opener pulled from the person, the body pulled from the company, the subject line was its own thing. I capped every email under 100 words because that's where reply rates hold up.

The part I care most about is the guardrails. A status check stops the AI from running on a record with missing data, and a second check makes sure no half-built email reaches the sequencer. If the data is bad, the record gets held, not sent.

One thing that came out of the scoring: 80% of the top accounts were already running ZoomInfo or Salesforce. That told me they had budget for sales tooling and shaped how I would position against what they already used.
