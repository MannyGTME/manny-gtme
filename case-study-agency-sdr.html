The Problem

Marketing agencies at the 10–50 headcount stage tend to grow entirely on referrals and inbound. Outbound is something they know they need but haven't formalized — until the referral pipeline slows, a key client churns, or a new service line needs to prove itself. The problem isn't that they don't want pipeline. It's that they haven't built the capacity to generate it.

The challenge was identifying which agencies were at that exact inflection point — not just a matching firmographic profile, but showing observable signals that commercial capacity was actively being built. The target was marketing agencies that were hiring for growth functions, expanding headcount, or launching new service lines: the three indicators that reliably precede a structured outbound motion.

The Workflow
  1. Built the core account universe in Clay — marketing agencies filtered by headcount range (8–50), service focus (performance, paid acquisition, demand gen, content), and geography. Starting list: ~600 accounts.
  2. Defined three scoring signals: net new hires in the last 60 days across any function, open roles in Sales, Business Development, or Growth, and a leadership hire in a commercial or client-facing function within the last 90 days.
  3. Enriched each record against LinkedIn for headcount velocity and hire data; pulled open role data from job board feeds via Clay. Scraped agency websites to identify current service lines and client verticals.
  4. Built a composite score across three dimensions: headcount growth velocity (0–3), active role signal (0–2), and service-line fit with the offer (0–1). Agencies scoring 4 or above entered the active sequence. Scores of 2–3 went into a lower-priority watch cadence.
  5. For each priority account, passed the top-scoring signal, agency context, and service-line data to Claude. The system selected the correct opening hook module and assembled a first touch referencing the specific signal — hire name, role title, or new service line — without manual lookup per record.
  6. Output: a weekly rolling send list of 30–50 prioritized agencies, refreshed as new signals fired. No static list — accounts aged out as signals became stale and new ones replaced them.

The Metrics

What I'd measure if this were running at full scale:

  • Signal accuracy — what percentage of triggered accounts are actively building outbound capacity at time of contact?
  • Score-to-meeting rate — do agencies scoring 5–6 convert at meaningfully higher rates than those scoring 3–4?
  • Role-signal lift — does an open BizDev or Sales role improve meeting rate over headcount growth alone?
  • Signal recency decay — how does reply rate change as the triggering signal ages from 0–2 weeks to 4–8 weeks?
