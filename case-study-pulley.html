Outbound

The Problem

Equity management platforms win deals at specific moments in a startup's lifecycle — usually around a funding event, when headcount is growing fast, or when cap table complexity starts outpacing a spreadsheet. Reaching a founder too early means the problem doesn't feel real yet. Too late means a competitor already has them locked in.

The challenge was building a targeting model that found startups at the exact right lifecycle stage, at volume, without requiring manual account research. The signal had to be specific enough to be actionable, and the outreach had to reference it clearly so the relevance was obvious.

The Workflow
  1. Defined the target lifecycle window: startups that had closed a Seed or Series A in the last 90 days, with 10–50 employees, and no existing equity management platform detected in their tech stack.
  2. Used Clay to pull funding event data, filtering on round type, close date, and headcount range. This produced the core trigger list.
  3. Layered in a secondary signal: active hiring in Finance, Legal, or HR roles — an indicator that operational infrastructure is being built out, which is exactly when cap table tooling becomes urgent.
  4. Scored each account across three dimensions: funding recency (decays weekly), headcount growth velocity, and hiring signal strength. Composite score determined send priority.
  5. For each record, Clay passed the funding event details, hire data, and company context to a Claude prompt that selected the right opening hook and assembled the email.
  6. High-priority accounts (top 20% by score) were routed directly into sequence. Mid-tier accounts triggered a slower follow-up cadence.

The Metrics

What I'd measure if this were running at full scale:

  • Timing window accuracy — what percentage of meetings booked came from accounts contacted within 60 days of their funding close?
  • Signal decay curve — how does reply rate change as funding recency increases from 30 to 90 to 180 days?
  • Hiring signal lift — does the secondary hire signal improve meeting rate vs. funding signal alone?
  • Lifecycle conversion — which combination of round size and headcount stage produces the highest close rate downstream?
